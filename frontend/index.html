What does this do?

It studies 11 years of Premier League results and uses that history to work out the chances of any fixture ending as a home win, draw, or away win, before a ball is kicked.

📊

It learned from history

The model was fed every Premier League match from 2015 to 2025, over 4,000 games. For each one it looked at recent form, goals scored, goals conceded, and the head-to-head record between the two clubs.

🔢

It found patterns

An algorithm went through all of that history and learned which patterns, like a team on a five-game winning run at home, tend to lead to which results. It weights recent form more heavily than old results.

⚖️

It gives chances, not verdicts

Football is hard to call. Instead of saying "Arsenal will win", it says "58% Arsenal, 22% draw, 20% Chelsea." Three numbers that always add up to 100%. The spread tells you how open the game looks.

🔄

It stays current

Every Monday and Thursday it pulls the latest results, retrains on the new data, and updates itself. The form figures it uses always reflect what has happened recently, not a snapshot from months ago.

Reading a prediction

Home Win 64%
In fixtures that look like this one, based on form and history, the home side wins roughly two times out of three.
Draw 21%
About one in five similar games ends level. Draws get their own number rather than being split between the two win probabilities.
Away Win 15%
Away teams win less often on average, but this number moves up or down based on how both teams have been playing lately.
Confidence 64%
This is just the highest of the three numbers. Above 70% the model is fairly sure; below 45% it sees the game as wide open.

What each tab does

Teams A full breakdown for any club: points per game, home and away record, goal difference, and their last five results.
